How it Works 🧬

Most friend groups fail to stay together because they all pick the exact same "popular" friend. This creates a bottleneck that the school's sorting algorithm can't solve, forcing it to split you up.

This simulator uses a mathematical approach called the Circular Daisy-Chain Strategy:

  1. It treats your group as a ring.
  2. Each person picks the friends directly following them in the circle (e.g., Person 1 picks 2, 3, 4. Person 2 picks 3, 4, 5).
  3. This creates a "redundant web" of connections. No matter which student the computer assigns to a room first, everyone else is mathematically pulled in with them.
